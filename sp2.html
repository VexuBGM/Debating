IMPORTANCE

Why is the Second speaker important

Provides a fresh perspective

As the debate progresses, the second speaker brings a fresh perspective to the discussion. They can introduce new angles, insights, or evidence that may not have been covered by the first speaker, offering a more comprehensive and persuasive case for their team.

Expands on the team's argument

The second speaker plays a crucial role in building upon the arguments presented by the first speaker. They provide additional evidence, analysis, and examples to strengthen the team's case and further develop their position.

Balance and Team Dynamics

In a professional debate, each team consists of multiple speakers who work together to present a cohesive argument. The second speaker acts as a bridge between the first and third speakers, maintaining the balance and continuity of the team's case. They ensure that the arguments flow smoothly and that there is a logical progression throughout the debate.

Things you can do to make a good impression

Rebuttal

It's important to rebbutal because it is an debate and the judges want to see you arguing with the opposite team

Road map

It makes a good impression to say how your speech is going to go. Say everything and make sure it is nice and organised.

Present all of your arguments

As the second speaker your role is to present all of your team's arguments and to defend them. You can do that by using examples

Sum up your speech

At the end of your speech you should say what you said in your speech.

TEMPLATE

How your speech should look like

1st step

Start with greeting.
Introduce yourself.
Then say your 3 arguments name by name but don't go into details.
Make a road map of your speech.

2nd step

The second speaker is responsible for anticipating and responding to the arguments put forth by the opposing team during their rebuttals. The second speaker need to effectively counter the opposing team's points and refute their claims, highlighting any flaws or weaknesses in their arguments.

3rd step

Get into details in your team's second and third argument. You need to defend them and that could happen if you use examples.
