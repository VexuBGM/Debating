IMPORTANCE

Why is the Third speaker important

Final Impact

As the last speaker for the team, the third speaker has the opportunity to leave a lasting impact on the judges and audience. They can deliver a strong, memorable closing statement that summarizes the team's main arguments, highlights their strengths, and makes a compelling case for their position. The final impression created by the third speaker can significantly influence the outcome of the debate.

Closing the Loop

The third speaker has the opportunity to tie together the team's arguments, creating a cohesive narrative. They can emphasize the interconnections between different points and show how they collectively strengthen the team's position. This closing of the loop enhances the team's overall argumentation and leaves a strong impression on the judges and audience.

Extension of Arguments

The third speaker builds upon the arguments presented by the previous speakers on their team. They can expand on the team's main points, provide additional evidence, and offer further analysis and examples. This extension of arguments adds depth and credibility to the team's case, reinforcing their position and persuasiveness.

Things you can do to make a good impression

Rebuttal

It's important to rebbutal because it is an debate and the judges want to see you arguing with the opposite team

Road map

It makes a good impression to say how your speech is going to go. Say everything and make sure it is nice and organised.

Present all of your and their arguments

As the third speaker your role is to present all of your team and your opposite team's arguments and to defend them.

Sum up your speech

At the end of your speech you should say what you said in your speech.

TEMPLATE

How your speech should look like

1st step

Start with greeting.
Introduce yourself.
Then say your 3 arguments name by name but don't go into details.
Make a road map of your speech.

2nd step

The second speaker is responsible for anticipating and responding to the arguments put forth by the opposing team during their rebuttals. The second speaker need to effectively counter the opposing team's points and refute their claims, highlighting any flaws or weaknesses in their arguments.

3rd step

Get into details in your team's second and third argument. You need to defend them and that could happen if you use examples.
